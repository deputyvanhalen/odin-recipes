Carbonara Recipe

Description

Pasta carbonara...or spaghetti carbonara if you're a traditionalist is a timeless classic. If you're going to make it, you need to make it right. So why not follow the New York Times recipe which does its best to be as authentic as possible. Sure you can use a different pasta, or pancetta instead of guanciale...but then what you have is NOT carbonara. When it comes to the five roman pastas, know the rules, follow the rules, and do NOT deviate. Otherwise you're not making the dish.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti. 16 oz.
  • Guanciale, diced. 5 oz.
  • Pecorino Romano Cheese. 8 oz.
  • Egg.
  • Black Pepper.

Steps

  • Bring water to boil in large pot.
  • In a pan, heat guanciale on low heat until browned, then remove.
  • Cook Pasta in water.
  • Whip eggs, cheese in a bowl into a paste using some pasta water to increase starch content.
  • When pasta is cooked, strain and throw into pan with guanciale.
  • Add in the paste and stir vigorously until emulsified sauce is formed
  • Serve and enjoy.
